üíé Deep Value Investing

Made famous by Warren Buffett & Benjamin Graham

Deep value investing focuses on finding stocks trading significantly below their intrinsic value. This approach looks for companies with strong fundamentals that the market has temporarily overlooked or undervalued. The goal is to buy "wonderful companies at fair prices" and hold them for the long term.

What We Look For

  • Low P/E ratios compared to industry peers
  • Strong balance sheets with low debt
  • Consistent dividend history
  • Wide economic moats (competitive advantages)
  • Quality management with shareholder-friendly practices
  • Companies trading below book value

Typical Holdings

  • Blue-chip dividend aristocrats
  • Consumer staples (Procter & Gamble, Coca-Cola)
  • Financial institutions (banks, insurance)
  • Healthcare giants (Johnson & Johnson)
Risk Level
Low-Medium
Time Horizon
5-10+ Years
Volatility
Lower

üöÄ Disruptive Innovation

Made famous by Cathie Wood & ARK Invest

Disruptive innovation investing targets companies developing breakthrough technologies that could fundamentally change industries. This high-conviction strategy accepts higher volatility in pursuit of exponential growth potential. The focus is on companies at the forefront of technological revolution.

What We Look For

  • Companies leading in AI, robotics, and automation
  • Genomics and biotechnology innovators
  • Electric vehicles and clean energy pioneers
  • Fintech disruptors and blockchain technology
  • Space exploration and next-gen internet
  • High revenue growth potential (30%+ annually)

Typical Holdings

  • AI and semiconductor leaders (NVIDIA)
  • Electric vehicle manufacturers (Tesla)
  • Genomics companies (CRISPR Therapeutics)
  • Data and cloud platforms (Palantir)
Risk Level
High
Time Horizon
5-7 Years
Volatility
Higher

üìà Growth at Reasonable Price (GARP)

Made famous by Peter Lynch & Fidelity Magellan Fund

GARP combines the best of growth and value investing. This approach seeks companies with above-average growth potential that aren't excessively overvalued. The strategy aims to find quality growth stocks before they become widely recognized, paying a reasonable price for future earnings potential.

What We Look For

  • PEG ratio (P/E to Growth) under 1.5
  • Consistent earnings growth (15-25% annually)
  • Strong return on equity (ROE)
  • Expanding profit margins
  • Market leadership in growing industries
  • Reasonable valuations relative to growth rate

Typical Holdings

  • Technology giants (Apple, Microsoft)
  • Financial services leaders (JPMorgan)
  • Healthcare innovators (UnitedHealth)
  • Established growth companies with proven track records
Risk Level
Medium
Time Horizon
3-5 Years
Volatility
Moderate
